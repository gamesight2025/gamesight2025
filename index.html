Towards Automatic Soccer Commentary Generation with Knowledge-Enhanced Visual Reasoning

Anonymous

Overview

The proposed system, called GameSight, incorporates visual and contextual information to generate soccer commentary that is entity-aligned and knowledge-enhanced.

Abstract

Soccer commentary plays a crucial role in enhancing the soccer game viewing experience for audiences. The automatic soccer commentary generation task, however, presents a challenge to the comprehensive narrative that integrates both coarse-grained event detection and fine-grained factual detail references. Previous studies typically adopt an end-to-end method that generates anonymous, context-free, and descriptive commentaries to explicate the game events. Such generated commentaries are clearly unnatural, as they lack the fine-grained visual and contextual information that human commentators possess for further insights. To bridge the gap, we propose GameSight, a two-stage model to address soccer commentary generation as a knowledge-enhanced visual reasoning task, enabling knowledgeable commentary with accurate reference to entities (players and teams). GameSight starts by performing visual reasoning to align anonymous entities with contextual background and fine-grained view shots analysis. Subsequently, the entity-aligned commentary is refined with knowledge by incorporating external historical statistics and iteratively updated internal game state information. Consequently, GameSight improves the player alignment accuracy by 26.8% on SN-Caption-test-align dataset compared to Gemini 2.0 Flash-Lite. Combined with further knowledge enhancement, GameSight outperforms in both local scale (segment-level) statistical accuracy and global scale (game-level) contextual relevance and structural composition. We believe that our work paves the way for a more informative and engaging human-centric experience with the AI sports application.



System Framework

Unlike previous end-to-end models, GameSight is a two-stage system that addresses soccer commentary generation as a knowledge-enhanced visual reasoning task. It corrects and refines the anonymized commentary with Stage I: visual reasoning for entity alignment, and Stage II: commentary refinement with knowledge.
